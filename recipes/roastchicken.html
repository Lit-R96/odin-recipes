ROAST CHICKEN

Home

Roasted chicken is a classic that combines simplicity with a deep, comforting flavor. The key to a perfect specimen lies in achieving a golden, crispy skin that protects a juicy and well-seasoned meat. By slow-cooking it in the oven, the natural juices caramelize, creating an irresistible texture that turns any ordinary meal into a banquet worthy of celebration.

This preparation stands out for its versatility, as it allows for play with a wide range of aromatic herbs and spices. By marinating the bird with compound butter or infused oils, every bite is imbued with earthy and citrus nuances. It is the ideal dish for those seeking a nutritious and elegant option that also fills the home with a warm, cozy aroma as it finishes browning in the tray.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 whole chicken (approximately 1.5 to 2 kg)
  • 50g butter
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 lemon (cut in half)
  • Fresh herbs: Rosemary, thyme, and bay leaf
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: Potatoes and carrots for the base of the tray

STEPS

  1. Initial Preparation: Pre-heat the oven to 200°C. Remove the chicken from the refrigerator about 20 minutes beforehand so it reaches room temperature. Pat it very dry with paper towels; dry skin is the secret to getting it crispy.
  2. Internal Seasoning: Generously salt and pepper the cavity of the chicken. Insert the lemon halves, two garlic cloves, and a bundle of fresh herbs (rosemary and thyme). This will aromatize the meat from the inside out.
  3. Making the Crust: In a small bowl, mix the butter with the remaining garlic, a bit of olive oil, salt, and pepper. Rub this mixture all over the outside of the bird, making sure to cover the thighs and wings well. If possible, slip a little butter under the skin of the breast.
  4. Arranging the Tray: Place the chicken breast-side up in a roasting pan. If you are using vegetables, place the chopped potatoes and carrots at the bottom so they cook in the juices released by the chicken.
  5. Roasting: Bake for approximately 60 to 75 minutes. Halfway through the process, you can baste the chicken with its own juices using a spoon. The chicken is ready when the juices run clear after piercing the thigh.
  6. Resting: Once out of the oven, let the chicken rest in the tray for 10 to 15 minutes before carving. This allows the fibers to relax and the juices to redistribute, ensuring it doesn't dry out when served.
